# How to handle a training-related incident

## Audience
Managers, safety users, HR users, and training coordinators.

## Purpose
Flag an incident for training review and coordinate follow-up without making StaffArr own the training assignment.

## Before You Start
- StaffArr access.
- Incident manage access.
- Person record, incident details, and training concern.
- TrainArr access if you also create or review retraining work.

## Steps
1. Open StaffArr.
2. Open the incident or create a new incident.
3. In the training or certification evaluation area, mark that training review may be required.
4. Describe the training concern clearly in the incident narrative.
5. Add the affected person and any related source record.
6. Submit or update the incident.
7. Open TrainArr remediation or assignments to create or review retraining work when the TrainArr follow-up is available.
8. Return to StaffArr to monitor the incident and person readiness status.

## What Happens Next
StaffArr records the incident and training concern. TrainArr owns the resulting retraining assignment and completion evidence.

## Troubleshooting
- If no TrainArr assignment appears, check whether the remediation handoff or manual assignment step is available for the tenant.
- Do not close the incident solely because retraining was requested; verify completion or the applicable follow-up state.
- If readiness is affected, review the person readiness display after TrainArr publishes the updated qualification result.
