# STLComplianceSite — Public Experience, Accessibility, Security, and Lead Handoff

## Public experience

The public site must explain the unified suite without presenting every product as an unrelated tool. Product pages use consistent structure, canonical logos, plain language, credible workflow examples, and one cross-suite narrative: operational data enters owning products, Compliance Core evaluates applicable requirements, and products receive actionable compliance results.

## Content and navigation

Use a restrained top navigation with Products, Industries, Platform, Resources, Trust, and Contact. Avoid route walls and repetitive product grids. Every page has a clear purpose, one primary conversion action, contextual related content, and professional light/dark rendering when theme selection is offered.

## Accessibility and performance

Meet the platform accessibility constitution for keyboard use, landmarks, heading order, forms, errors, motion, contrast, and responsive behavior. Set performance budgets for initial JavaScript, images, fonts, and layout shift. Marketing animation may not delay content or block reduced-motion users.

## Security and privacy

Public forms are rate-limited, spam-resistant, size-limited, CSRF/abuse protected as applicable, and do not expose internal APIs or diagnostics. Consent, privacy notices, retention, and communication preferences are captured explicitly. Do not collect operational, regulated, credential, or evidence files through a generic lead form.

## Lead handoff

The site owns public intake until accepted by CustomArr. Submission creates an auditable handoff with consent/source/campaign/context, duplicate hints, and idempotency. CustomArr owns the lead/customer lifecycle after acceptance. Failed handoff enters a durable review queue and the visitor receives only truthful confirmation of what was actually accepted.

## Trust and status

Security, privacy, legal, accessibility, terms, support boundaries, and service status use governed content with review dates and publication history. Claims must match implemented controls and may not overstate certifications, compliance guarantees, uptime, or production readiness.
