# How to handle delays, missed stops, or exceptions

## Audience
Drivers, dispatchers, and transportation supervisors

## Product
RoutArr

## Support Status
Supported by current UI/API

## Purpose
Record transportation exceptions so dispatch, customers, warehouse, and reporting have accurate status.

## Before You Start
- RoutArr owns transportation exceptions.
- Other products own source facts such as vehicle readiness, load readiness, customer context, and compliance blockers.

## Steps
1. Open RoutArr.
2. Open Exceptions, Trips, Driver portal, or Dispatch board.
3. Select the trip, stop, or route affected.
4. Choose the exception or status action available for the situation.
5. Classify the issue as delay, missed stop, damaged freight, breakdown, blocker, or the closest supported option.
6. Add notes, timing, proof, and related product references.
7. Save the exception.
8. Coordinate follow-up with MaintainArr, LoadArr, StaffArr, TrainArr, Compliance Core, or RecordArr when their records are involved.

## What Happens Next
RoutArr records the exception and may update trip status, stop state, customer visibility, and reporting.

## Troubleshooting
- If the issue affects asset readiness, open MaintainArr.
- If the issue affects load or inventory state, open LoadArr.
- If evidence is required, store or attach it through RecordArr.
