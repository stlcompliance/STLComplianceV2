# How to report an incident

## Audience
Managers, safety users, HR, and authorized staff

## Product
StaffArr

## Support Status
Supported by current UI/API

## Purpose
Capture a personnel-facing incident and determine whether readiness, restrictions, training review, or evidence are needed.

## Before You Start
- StaffArr owns personnel incident history.
- The originating product still owns its own operational record.
- Use RecordArr for stored evidence when evidence capture is required.

## Steps
1. Open StaffArr.
2. Open Incidents.
3. Choose Create.
4. Complete Incident Basics, including source, type, severity, date, and summary.
5. Add involved people in People and Involvement.
6. Write the narrative in Narrative and Details.
7. Set readiness or restriction impact when the incident changes work authority.
8. Use the training review section when retraining or certification evaluation may be needed.
9. Add related records or cross-product references when the incident came from maintenance, route, supply, training, compliance, or another product.
10. Add evidence details when available.
11. Review notifications and workflow settings.
12. Choose Save Draft if more information is needed, or Submit Incident when the report is ready.

## What Happens Next
Submitted incidents become StaffArr incident records. Training-related incidents can be marked for TrainArr remediation review, and evidence can be connected through RecordArr.

## Troubleshooting
- If you cannot submit, check required fields in each section.
- If the incident should trigger training, verify the training impact section was completed.
- If evidence upload is not available on the incident screen, create or attach the document through RecordArr.
