# How to run a quality audit and findings

## Audience
Quality auditors, quality managers, process owners, and finding reviewers

## Product
AssurArr

## Support Status
Supported by current UI/API

## Purpose
Plan a quality audit, execute checklist work, create findings, and verify closure inside AssurArr.

## Before You Start
- AssurArr owns quality audits, audit findings, finding status, and verification.
- RecordArr owns retained audit evidence files.
- Target products own corrective execution when a finding assigns work outside AssurArr.

## Steps
1. Open AssurArr.
2. Open Audits.
3. Select Create audit.
4. Enter audit type, scope, lead auditor, auditors, planned dates, site, location, standards, requirements, and auditees.
5. Create the audit and open the detail page.
6. Add or open audit checklists.
7. Complete checklist items and attach evidence records where required.
8. Create findings for failed, questionable, or improvement items.
9. Open Findings to classify each finding and link nonconformance or CAPA records when needed.
10. Verify finding closure after corrective work and evidence are complete.
11. Close the audit when findings and required verification work are complete.

## What Happens Next
AssurArr keeps audit and finding history. Corrective work, documents, training, maintenance, inventory, supplier, customer, or order follow-up remains owned by the product responsible for that domain.

## Troubleshooting
- If checklist evidence is missing, attach it through RecordArr-backed capture.
- If a finding needs operational correction, route the action to the owning product instead of closing the finding with a note.
- If a finding repeats, link the prior nonconformance or CAPA so recurrence is visible.
