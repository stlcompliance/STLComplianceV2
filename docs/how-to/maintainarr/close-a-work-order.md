# How to close a work order

## Audience
Technicians, maintenance supervisors, and maintenance admins

## Product
MaintainArr

## Support Status
Supported by current UI/API

## Purpose
Complete maintenance work with labor, parts, evidence, and closeout notes.

## Before You Start
- The work order must exist in MaintainArr.
- Parts issue and stock movement remain owned by LoadArr.
- Documents and evidence should be stored through RecordArr when required.

## Steps
1. Open MaintainArr.
2. Open Work orders and select the work order.
3. Review tasks, assigned labor, parts demand, defects, inspections, and evidence requirements.
4. Record completed work, labor, parts used, and notes.
5. Upload or attach required evidence when the workflow provides that action.
6. Confirm parts fulfillment and installed quantities are correct.
7. Resolve related defects or readiness blockers when appropriate.
8. Use the close or complete action.
9. Review the final work order status and history.

## What Happens Next
MaintainArr records the closed work order, updates asset history, and may update asset readiness. RecordArr can store supporting evidence and closeout documents.

## Troubleshooting
- If the work order cannot close, check missing evidence, unresolved defects, incomplete inspections, or parts demand state.
- If stock usage is wrong, correct it through LoadArr workflows rather than editing inventory inside MaintainArr.
